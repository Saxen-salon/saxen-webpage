## 1. Core Messaging Hierarchy

### Primary Message

**"Du ved hvad du betaler — inden du overhovedet sætter dig i stolen."**

Saxen publishes a complete, line-by-line price list covering 35+ services. This is the single most differentiating thing about the salon's public presence. Most Danish hair salons do not publish prices at this level of transparency. Lead every channel with it. The primary message is not "great hair" — it is the confidence that comes from knowing exactly what a visit costs before you arrive.

This is the homepage hero claim. It is specific to Saxen. No other salon in Hjørring makes it credibly.

### Secondary Messages (3–4 supporting points)

1. **Et hold af seks erfarne frisører — ikke en enkeltmand med skiftende vikarer.**
   Saxen has six named staff with confirmed faces. A stable team means repeat customers get the same hands. This matters enormously to women who build a relationship with one stylist over years.

2. **Alt under ét tag — fra babylugt til brudefrisure.**
   The service range covers the entire family and every occasion: baby/children's cuts, men's cuts and beard trims, full color and highlights, perms, formal bridal/confirmation styling, and brow/lash treatments. No one in the household needs to go elsewhere.

3. **Farve og highlights? Ring og snak med os først — det er god service, ikke besværlighed.**
   Color bookings require a phone call. This is rare and can be positioned as a quality signal: color outcomes depend on a real conversation, not a click. Frame it as care, not friction.

4. **Midt i Hjørring — nem parkering, korte åbningstider der matcher en travl hverdag.**
   Location on Jernbanegade (central Hjørring), opening hours Monday–Friday 09:00–17:30 and Saturday 08:00–13:00 serve working adults and weekend shoppers alike.

## 2. Tone of Voice

### Formality Level

**Warm and direct — Danish informal throughout.** Use "du" and "vi" without hesitation. Never "De". Short sentences. No subordinate clause chains. Read every sentence aloud — if it sounds like a corporate press release, rewrite it.

Danish salons trend toward warm and personal, and Saxen should lean fully into this. The existing welcome text ("Vi glæder os til at se dig her i salonen") is exactly the right register — the redesign amplifies and makes it more specific, but never loses that warmth.

### Voice Attributes

**Ærlig (Honest)**
Saxen says what things cost. The copy follows the same principle: direct statements, no hyperbole, no vague claims. "Vi er gode til farvebehandling" is stronger than "world-class colourists".

**Nær (Close/Personal)**
Six names, six faces. The copy speaks person-to-person — not brand-to-consumer. Use first names where natural. Avoid abstractions like "our team of professionals."

**Tryg (Reassuring/Safe)**
No surprises. You know the price. You know who cuts your hair. You can book online or call. Every friction point is resolved before it becomes an anxiety.

**Lokal (Local, rooted)**
Hjørring is not a backdrop — it is the identity. Saxen is not a brand that happens to have a location; it is a Hjørring salon. Reference the city by name. This differentiates from any chain that could be anywhere.

### What NOT to Sound Like

Avoid these patterns absolutely:

- "Vi er lidenskabelige for hår" — "passion for hair" is said by every salon on earth and means nothing
- "En luksuriøs oplevelse" — Saxen's price range and location are mid-market local; luxury positioning would read as dishonest
- "Vores dygtige team" without specifics — if you want to establish expertise, name someone or cite a service
- "Book din tid i dag!" with exclamation marks — pressure-selling is the opposite of Saxen's calm, trustworthy register
- "Professionelle resultater" — this is the baseline expectation, not a differentiator

### Sample Phrases in Danish

These three sentences demonstrate the correct tone — use them as a calibration reference when writing any copy for the site:

1. **Hero / trust:** "Hos Saxen finder du altid prisen på forhånd — ingen overraskelser, ingen ubehagelige regninger."

2. **Team / personal:** "Vi er seks frisører med mange års erfaring, og de fleste af vores kunder kender os ved navn."

3. **CTA / inviting:** "Har du spørgsmål til en farvebehandling, så ring til os — vi hjælper dig gerne med at finde det rigtige."

## 3. Audience Segments

### Primary: Local women, 30–60, Hjørring and surrounding area

**Who they are:** Repeat customers who have an established relationship with one stylist. They may have been going to the same salon for years. Switching is a high-stakes decision.

**Decision criteria:**
- Consistency — the same stylist, reliably, for the same result
- Price visibility — they want to know the bill before sitting down
- Range — they want cuts, color, and styling in one place without driving to Aalborg

**Anxieties:**
- New stylist ruins a color they've had for years
- Hidden costs added at checkout ("plus product, plus treatment...")
- Booking a new salon and not knowing what to expect

**What Saxen offers them:** Named, photographed staff; a stable team with declared experience; a full public price list; and an explicitly personal consultation approach.

### Secondary: Local men, 25–55

**Who they are:** Functional, infrequent visitors. They want a clean cut at a fair price with minimal friction.

**Decision criteria:** Price (355 kr for a men's cut), speed, and convenience

**Anxieties:** Booking feeling complicated; being upsold; waiting too long

**What Saxen offers them:** A flat published price, online booking, and a centrally located salon.

### Tertiary: Families with children

**Who they are:** Parents who want one reliable salon for the whole family. Trust is inherited — if mum has been going for years, the children follow.

**Decision criteria:** Child-friendly atmosphere, known pricing (100 kr for baby cuts), proximity

**Anxieties:** A child having a bad experience with an impatient stylist

**What Saxen offers them:** Baby cut as a published line item (signals experience with children); stable staff who will be recognised on the next visit.

### Occasional: Special occasion / event clients

**Who they are:** Brides, confirmation families, women preparing for a formal event. Often one-time or rare visitors with a high emotional investment in the result.

**Decision criteria:** Confidence in the stylist's ability to deliver a specific look; price transparency for what are relatively large single transactions (960 kr bridal, 660 kr confirmation updo)

**Anxieties:** Not getting what they asked for; the salon having no experience with formal styling

**What Saxen offers them:** Published occasion prices signal experience and confidence in delivery; a phone-first booking process for color/complex work also reassures this segment.

## 4. Messaging Rules

### Lead with

- **Price transparency** — it is Saxen's strongest differentiator and the most immediately useful thing a new customer can find
- **The team by name** — six real people with real photos, not stock imagery; this is a local salon and that is a virtue
- **The full service range** — reassure prospective customers they don't need to go elsewhere for anything
- **Location and hours** — local customers decide on practicality first; make these impossible to miss

### Bury (or omit entirely)

- Generic quality claims: "high quality", "professionelt", "dygtige frisører" — assume these as baseline; saying them wastes space
- Vague experience claims without anchoring detail: "years of experience" is weak; "several years collectively, serving Hjørring families" is better
- Booking friction descriptions — mention online booking positively; only explain the phone requirement for color as a quality signal, never as an apology
- Anything that implies Saxen is aspirational or upmarket — the price range and setting are solidly mid-market; own that honestly

### Keywords to use

- **frisør Hjørring** — primary local search term; must appear in page titles and meta descriptions
- **priser** / **prisliste** — the differentiator; link to the price page prominently in navigation
- **farve og highlights** — the highest-demand service category for the primary female audience
- **brudefrisure** / **opsætning** — occasion terms; should appear in service descriptions
- **online booking** — a concrete action signal for search and CTA copy
- The six first names: Susanne, Anita, Heidi, Tina, Merete, Camilla — real staff names in context build trust and local SEO relevance

### Keywords to avoid

- "luksus", "eksklusivt", "premium" — misleading positioning for this price bracket
- "passion for hår", "håreksperter", "mestre" — overused salon clichés with no informational value
- "world-class", "state-of-the-art" — jarring in Danish local context and patently unverifiable
- "oplevelse" used as a standalone noun — too vague; always pair it with a specific ("en tryg farvebehandling" not "en unik oplevelse")

## 5. Page-by-Page Messaging Notes

### Home

The homepage's job is to answer "why Saxen, not the salon down the street?" in under 10 seconds for a first-time visitor arriving from a Google search for "frisør Hjørring". Lead the hero with the price transparency claim and a direct CTA to the price list or booking. The secondary section establishes the team as real, local, experienced people — names and faces above the fold on desktop. The tertiary conversion action is newsletter signup or a clear phone number for color inquiries. Do not overload the homepage with service detail — that belongs on the Services page.

**Key conversion action:** Book online / call for color inquiry

### Services

The services page's job is to confirm that Saxen covers whatever the visitor needs, and to make prices findable immediately adjacent to each service category. Group services logically: Women (klip, farve, highlights, opsætning), Men (klip, skæg), Children (babylugt, børneklip), Occasions (brude, konfirmation), Treatments (Wella Plex, bryn, vipper). Each group should include the price range from the published list. This page also handles the color inquiry — include a clear "Ring til os for farve og highlights" callout with the phone number so the hand-off is frictionless.

**Key conversion action:** Navigate to booking / call for color

### Staff / Team

The team page's job is to reduce the anxiety of visiting a new salon. Six names and faces, confirming this is not a revolving door of temporary staff. If client provides bios, specialisms, and tenure information, this becomes a major trust-builder. Without that data, the page still works with names, photos, and a one-line description per person. The tone should be warm and introductory — "meet the people who will look after you."

**Key conversion action:** Reduce pre-visit anxiety; does not need a hard CTA beyond the booking link in the site header

### Prices

The prices page is the most strategically important page on the site after the homepage. It is a competitive differentiator and should be treated as a first-class destination, not an afterthought. Present the full price list clearly, grouped by service category. Add a short introductory paragraph that reframes the list as a statement of trust: "Vi har altid haft åbne priser — det synes vi er den mest ærlige måde at drive frisørsalon på." Make the page scannable for mobile (most visitors will check prices on their phones before booking). Include the phone number and booking CTA at the bottom.

**Key conversion action:** Build pre-booking confidence; direct to booking or phone CTA

### Contact

The contact page's job is zero friction. Address, phone number, opening hours, and the booking link must all be immediately visible without scrolling. Add a simple embedded map (Jernbanegade 1, Hjørring). Include the color/highlight caveat: "Ønsker du farve eller highlights, bedes du ringe til os, så vi kan aftale det bedste resultat for dig." Frame this as care. Include Susanne's email (susanne@karlborg.dk) if the client confirms this is still the correct general inquiry address — or request a generic contact@saxen.dk address for cleaner branding.

**Key conversion action:** Phone call, online booking, or form submission
