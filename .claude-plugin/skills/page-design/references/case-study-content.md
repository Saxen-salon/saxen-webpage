# Case Studies — Content Requirements

## Case Studies Listing Page

### Page Goal
Provide evidence of successful delivery across industries and project types. Let visitors filter to find relevant examples.

### Required Content Elements

| Element | Priority | Content Needed |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Case study cards | Required | Title, industry, key metric, image, link to full case |
| Industry filtering | Recommended | Let visitors find cases relevant to their industry |
| Results emphasis | Required | Make outcomes visible from the listing, not just titles |

---

## Individual Case Study Page

### Page Goal
Prove the company delivered real results on a real project. The most convincing content on any B2B website.

### Required Content Elements

| Element | Priority | Content Needed |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Client / project context | Required | Who the client is (if shareable), what industry, what challenge they faced |
| Challenge | Required | The specific problem or need — make it relatable to the reader |
| Solution | Required | What the company did — specific services, approach, technical decisions |
| Results | Required | Quantifiable outcomes — numbers, improvements, metrics |
| Client testimonial | Recommended | Direct quote from the client (extremely high-trust signal) |
| Project specs | Recommended | Technical details that demonstrate capability |
| Related services | Recommended | Links to the services used in this project |
| CTA | Required | "Have a similar project?" conversion prompt |

### Content Framework

```
CHALLENGE: What was the client's situation and pain point?
SOLUTION:  What specific approach did the company take?
RESULTS:   What measurable outcomes were achieved?
```

Each section should be 2-4 sentences minimum. Vague case studies ("we helped them improve quality") are worse than no case study at all.
